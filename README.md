# Korean AI Compliance Readiness Assessment

A 12-week industry-sponsored research project investigating how Korean AI companies navigate Western regulatory requirements (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF), combining technical AI safety research with cross-cultural regulatory translation.

For a discussion on the importance of regulation based on the author’s other research, please refer to the document below.
[View Document] (Korean-ai-research/Why Current AI Safety Frameworks Miss a Critical Attack Vector.pdf)

## Overview

The global AI regulatory landscape is rapidly evolving, with the EU AI Act and NIST AI Risk Management Framework setting new standards for AI safety and governance. Korean AI companies seeking to expand into Western markets face significant compliance challenges but lack accessible, technically-grounded guidance.

This project sits at the intersection of technical AI safety research, cross-cultural regulatory translation, and real-world market validation.

## Project Structure

### Phase 1 — Market Mapping (Weeks 1-2)

Build a database of 20 qualified Korean AI companies meeting the following criteria: Series A+ funding or established enterprise revenue, active LLM/generative AI development, stated interest in US/EU market expansion, and 10-100 employees.

**Deliverable:** Spreadsheet database with company profiles, key personnel, funding stage, compliance posture, and pain score (1-10)

---

### Phase 2 — Outreach & Technical Assessments (Weeks 3-8)

Conduct 60-90 minute video calls with technical leadership at 15+ companies, covering company background, compliance current state, technical deep-dive, gap identification, and needs assessment.

Each company receives a customized report within one week of their call, including an executive summary in Korean, technical findings, prioritized recommendations, and next steps.

**Lead Qualification Score (post-call)**

| Dimension | Score (1-10) |
|-----------|-------------|
| Need | Severity of compliance gap |
| Urgency | Timeline for solving it |
| Budget | Ability to pay for help |
| Authority | Seniority of contact |

Companies scoring 7+ across all four dimensions are flagged as hot leads.

**Deliverable:** 15+ completed assessments with individual written reports

---

### Phase 3 — Aggregate Analysis & Publication (Weeks 9-12)

Synthesize all assessment data into a bilingual research report titled *"The State of AI Governance Readiness in Korean Startups: A Technical Analysis"*, covering aggregate compliance scores, common gaps, market sizing, EU AI Act readiness by risk category, NIST RMF implementation gaps, and 2-3 anonymized case studies.

**Distribution:** LinkedIn (bilingual), Korean tech media (GeekNews, 44bits), AI governance conferences, gated download as lead magnet

**Deliverable:** Publishable research report + slide deck

## Ethical Guidelines

This project operates at the intersection of research and business development. The following principles apply throughout:

- **Transparency:** Always identify as a researcher; never misrepresent the commercial aspect
- **Value delivery:** Every company receives a genuine, useful assessment regardless of sales potential
- **Confidentiality:** All company-specific information is anonymized in the public report unless explicitly permitted
- **Academic rigor:** Findings must be honest and unbiased, even if they contradict business assumptions
- **No pressure:** Companies are never pressured to buy services
